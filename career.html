Prince

Career

Prince Rogers was an American singer, songwriter, musician, record producer, actor and filmmaker. A prominent music figure of the 1980s, Prince was known for his eclectic work, flamboyant stage presence, extravagant fashion sense and use of makeup, and wide vocal range. His band "The Revolution" collaborators
A multi-instrumentalist, he was considered a guitar virtuoso and was also skilled at playing the drums, percussion, bass, keyboards, and synthesizer. He would frequently play every instrument on his albums.

His most famous albums include "Prince", "1999", and Purple Rain, the soundtrack album to his film debut. It quickly became his most critically and commercially successful release, spending 24 consecutive weeks atop the Billboard 200 and selling over 20 million copies worldwide.

Awards

  • Eight Grammy Awards
  • Six American Music Awards
  • An Academy Award for the 1984 film Purple Rain.
  • At one point in 1984, Prince simultaneously had the No. 1 album, single, and film in the US; the; first time a singer had achieved this feat.
